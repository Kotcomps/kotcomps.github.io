Global Economic Outlook: World Bank Warns of Growth Slowing to 2.5% in 2026

Inflation and Trade Tensions Drag Growth

The World Bank released its latest global economic forecast today, warning that persistent inflation and escalating trade policy disputes are projected to slow global GDP growth to just 2.5% for 2026.

The report highlights that high debt levels are severely limiting development spending in low-income nations, while energy price volatility continues to disrupt supply chains in major industrial hubs.

"A prolonged period of sluggish growth could set back development goals by a decade. Structural reforms are urgently needed to restore confidence," the World Bank Chief Economist warned.

The report calls on central banks to coordinate monetary policies and warns against the rising use of strategic tariffs, which could further restrict international trade.
