Trump Calls Off Iran Attack at Request of Gulf States

Postponement at the Eleventh Hour

In a dramatic reversal of US foreign policy, President Donald Trump has postponed a planned military strike on Iran. The strike, which was scheduled for Tuesday night in response to the downing of a US surveillance drone, was called off following urgent requests from Gulf State allies, including Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates.

"We were cocked and loaded to retaliate last night," Trump posted on social media. "But at the request of our friends in the Gulf, who believe serious negotiations are now taking place, I have decided to give diplomacy another chance."

A Region on the Edge

The Middle East remains at a critical crossroads. While the temporary pause in military action has brought a sigh of relief to global energy markets, experts warn that the risk of conflict remains high. Gulf allies are reportedly concerned that a direct US strike could lead to retaliatory attacks on their own oil infrastructure and destabilize the region further.

Diplomatic channels are now working overtime, with European intermediaries attempting to broker direct talks between Washington and Tehran. However, Iranian officials have stated they will not negotiate under the threat of sanctions, leaving the final outcome of this geopolitical standoff highly uncertain.
