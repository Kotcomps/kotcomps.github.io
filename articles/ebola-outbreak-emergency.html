Ebola Outbreak Declared International Emergency — 131 Dead

WHO Sound the Alarm

The World Health Organization (WHO) has officially declared the ongoing Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. The outbreak, centered in the volatile eastern province of North Kivu, has already claimed at least 131 lives out of 210 confirmed cases, representing a high mortality rate.

"This is a highly concerning outbreak involving a rare strain in a conflict zone," said WHO Director-General Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus. "The combination of active militia violence and community distrust makes this one of the most challenging health emergencies we have faced in years."

Navigating a Conflict Zone

Eastern DRC is home to dozens of active rebel groups, making it extremely dangerous for health workers to track contacts and administer vaccines. Several clinics have already been attacked, forcing temporary suspensions of medical operations. International health agencies are calling for immediate security corridors to protect medical personnel and patients.

The WHO is working closely with neighboring countries, including Uganda and Rwanda, to step up border screening and prevent the virus from spreading across international borders. Global health experts warn that without immediate international funding and security support, the outbreak could rapidly spiral out of control, threatening the entire region.
