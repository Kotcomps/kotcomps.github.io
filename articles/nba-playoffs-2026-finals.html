NBA Playoffs 2026: Who's Making the Finals?

A Postseason of Pure Chaos

The 2026 NBA Playoffs have been nothing short of spectacular, delivering upset after upset and rewriting the postseason narrative. As we head into the Conference Finals, the usual title favorites have been eliminated, leaving a group of hungry, young teams competing for the Larry O'Brien Trophy.

"This is the most wide-open playoff field we've seen in a decade," says NBA analyst Doris Burke. "There is no clear favorite, which makes every game a must-watch event."

The Rise of the Underdogs

The biggest story of the postseason has been the incredible play of San Antonio's young core, led by Victor Wembanyama, who has elevated his game to MVP-level heights in the playoffs. However, they face a formidable challenge in the Thunder, whose deep roster and perimeter defense make them a nightmare matchup. In the East, the Celtics' experience is being tested by a fast-paced, high-scoring underdog that nobody expected to make it past the first round.

With multiple games going into overtime and rookie players stepping up in clutch moments, the 2026 playoffs are proving that star power alone is no longer enough to secure a championship. Team chemistry, defensive adaptability, and coaching adjustments are deciding who survives and who goes home.
